# RV Trip Itinerary

Starting location: Ontario, NY, USA

This round trip begins and ends in Ontario, New York, heading south through Ohio and Tennessee, crossing Alabama to the Gulf Coast, then looping back north through Atlanta and Chattanooga before a longer final push home through western New York. The itinerary emphasizes pet-friendly stops, beach time, waterfalls, museums, local food, and amenity-rich camping from the Gulf portion onward. Total distance is approximately 2,435 miles.

## Route Overview

Ontario, NY -> Cambridge, OH -> Grimsley, TN -> Cookeville, TN -> Birmingham, AL -> Gulf State Park, AL -> Pensacola Beach, FL -> Fort Walton Beach, FL -> Stone Mountain / Atlanta, GA -> Chattanooga, TN -> Rochester / Ontario, NY

## Day 1

- Route: Ontario, NY -> Cambridge, OH
- Dinner: Theo's Restaurant
  Classic family diner serving hearty American meals, including local favorites and hot dogs.
- Activity: Salt Fork State Park Waterfall Trail
  A pleasant three-mile trail featuring several cascades and a chance for wild swimming near the falls.
- Activity: Mosser Glass Company
  Tour a historic glass factory and browse their retail shop for unique gifts.

## Day 2

- Route: Cambridge, OH -> Grimsley, TN
- Dinner: Kristina's Kitchen
  Home-cooked plates and classic grilled hot dogs in a welcoming roadside cafe.
- Activity: Big South Fork National River & Recreation Area
  Explore dramatic sandstone bluffs, wild swimming, and moderate hiking trails.
- Activity: Sergeant Alvin C. York State Historic Park
  Walk in the footsteps of the famed WWI hero and enjoy history-focused trail tours.

## Day 3

- Route: Grimsley, TN -> Cookeville, TN
- Dinner: Dixie Lee Diner
  Cozy place for Southern comfort food and regional specialties, dog-friendly patio.
- Activity: Honey Creek Loop Trail
  Challenging hike with waterfalls, wild swimming holes, and lush forest scenery in Big South Fork.
- Activity: Antique Market of Cookeville
  Browse a treasure trove of unique finds and antiques from local artisans.

## Day 4

- Route: Cookeville, TN -> Birmingham, AL
- Dinner: SAW's Soul Kitchen
  Local hotspot known for innovative barbecue, hot dogs, and southern-style sides.
- Activity: Vulcan Park and Museum
  Catch panoramic city views and local history from atop the Vulcan statue.
- Activity: Birmingham Museum of Art
  Explore diverse collections of American, European, Asian, and folk art in an expansive downtown gallery.

## Day 5

- Route: Birmingham, AL
- Dinner: Hot Diggity Dogs
  Renowned for gourmet hot dogs and craft sodas in a funky, pet-friendly venue downtown.
- Activity: Alabama Jazz Hall of Fame
  Dive into Alabama's musical heritage with interactive exhibits and vintage instruments.
- Activity: Railroad Park Foundation
  Enjoy a scenic urban stroll with wetland boardwalks, skyline views, and lush lawns that are good for dogs.

## Day 6

- Route: Birmingham, AL -> Gulf State Park, AL
- Accommodation: Gulf State Park Campground
  Waterfront RV-friendly, pet-friendly site in a renowned state park with electric hookups and quick access to the beach trail system.
- Dinner: The Gulf
  Trendy open-air restaurant with fresh seafood, signature hot dogs, and views of the bay.
- Activity: Bon Secour National Wildlife Refuge
  Gentle beach hike and birdwatching along quiet sandy trails, with leashed dogs welcome in select areas.
- Activity: Gulf Adventure Center at Gulf State Park
  Zipline or eco-kayak options with shaded dog-friendly waiting areas.

## Day 7

- Route: Gulf State Park, AL -> Pensacola Beach, FL
- Accommodation: Pensacola Beach RV Resort
  Waterfront RV park with all amenities, direct bay beach access, and pet-friendly play zones.
- Dinner: Red Fish Blue Fish
  Beachfront spot offering inventive hot dogs, seafood, a dog-friendly patio, and live music at sunset.
- Activity: Gulf Islands National Seashore - Opal Beach
  White sand beaches, wild swimming, and short scenic hikes with smooth paved access from FL-399.
- Activity: Shoreline Park
  Boardwalks and quiet nature trails through coastal preserves, ideal for birdwatching and morning runs.

## Day 8

- Route: Pensacola Beach, FL -> Fort Walton Beach, FL
- Accommodation: Destin West RV Resort
  Premium, amenity-rich, pet-friendly waterfront RV park near Okaloosa Island with pool and bay access.
- Dinner: Stewby's Seafood Shanty
  Local icon for Gulf seafood and regional favorites in a laid-back setting.
- Activity: John Beasley Park
  Beach walks, wild swimming, and shaded areas for dog-friendly picnics.
- Activity: Okaloosa Island Pier
  Gulf views, dolphin watching, photo stops, and a lively fishing scene.

## Day 9

- Route: Fort Walton Beach, FL -> Stone Mountain / Atlanta, GA
- Accommodation: Stone Mountain Park Campground
  Waterfront, pet-friendly, shaded RV campground with easy access to Atlanta.
- Dinner: The Varsity
  Legendary fast food emporium known for chili dogs and Atlanta-style hot dogs since 1928.
- Activity: Martin Luther King Jr. National Historical Park
  Tour the historic home, church, and exhibits to learn about the civil-rights legacy. Grounds are dog-friendly.
- Activity: Ponce City Market
  Shop, eat, and explore the renovated Sears building with food halls, local shops, and rooftop amusements.

## Day 10

- Route: Stone Mountain / Atlanta, GA
- Accommodation: Stone Mountain Park Campground
  Continue at the same amenity-rich, dog-friendly campsite among wooded parkland.
- Dinner: Fox Bros. Bar-B-Q
  Massively popular Texas-style barbecue with a strong local twist.
- Activity: World of Coca-Cola
  Explore the history and pop-culture impact of Coca-Cola and taste global varieties.
- Activity: Atlanta BeltLine Eastside Trail
  Walk or run this art-filled urban trail connecting parks, shops, and dog-friendly spaces.

## Day 11

- Route: Stone Mountain / Atlanta, GA -> Chattanooga, TN
- Accommodation: Chester Frost Park Campground
  Pet-friendly, waterfront county park with roomy RV sites along Chickamauga Lake.
- Dinner: Champy's Famous Fried Chicken
  Vibrant Southern stop known for big flavor and live-music energy.
- Activity: Lookout Mountain Incline Railway
  Historic funicular ride with panoramic views from the St. Elmo depot.
- Activity: Coolidge Park
  Dog-friendly lawns and skyline views connected to the Tennessee Riverwalk.

## Day 12

- Route: Chattanooga, TN -> Rochester / Ontario, NY
- Dinner: Bernunzio Uptown Music
  Historic music store with world-class instruments and live events to close the adventure.
- Activity: Letchworth State Park
  Detour via NY-63 and NY-36 to the "Grand Canyon of the East," with three major waterfalls, overlooks, and RV-friendly paved roads.
- Activity: George Eastman Museum
  Tour America's first photography museum and mansion, then walk the gardens and galleries.

## Notes

- Every night after April 17 is described as waterfront, pet-friendly, and amenity-rich camping.
- The early portion of the trip uses free overnight camping through the major cities.
